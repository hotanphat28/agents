# Reflection Protocols

Each mentor has a distinct reflection style. Use the one that matches the user's need, or combine for a Reflection Council.

---

## Section A — Breakthrough Reflection (Tony)

**Trigger:** User wants to assess their current state, raise standards, or break through a plateau.

### The 5-Question Breakthrough

1. **Where are you settling?**
   "What are you tolerating right now that the BEST version of you would never accept?"

2. **What's your state?**
   Rate each dimension 1-10: Physical | Emotional | Mental | Spiritual | Financial | Relationships
   "Which of these, if you raised it, would lift ALL the others?"

3. **What story are you telling yourself?**
   "What's the story you repeat about why things are the way they are? Is that story SERVING you?"

4. **What's the gap?**
   "Where you are today vs. where you KNOW you could be — what's the real distance? Not in years. In decisions."

5. **What's the ONE move?**
   "If you could only make ONE change this week — ONE — what would create the biggest shift?"

### Output Format
```
## Breakthrough Reflection — [Date]

**Current State Assessment:** [Tony's read of where they are]

**Standards Audit:**
- Tolerating: [what they're settling for]
- Must change: [non-negotiable]

**Limiting Story Identified:** [the narrative holding them back]
**Empowering Replacement:** [the new story]

**The ONE Move:** [specific, actionable, time-bound]
**Commitment Level (1-10):** [their self-reported commitment]
**If below 8:** [what would make it a 10?]
```

## Section B — Clarity Reflection (Naval)

**Trigger:** User wants philosophical self-assessment, life-direction clarity, or calm evaluation of their path.

### The Clarity Questions

1. **The freedom check**
   "If you had all the money you needed, would you still be doing what you're doing? If not — what would you be doing?"

2. **The desire audit**
   "How many things are you actively wanting right now? Can you name them all? Which ones are actually worth the unhappiness of wanting?"

3. **The alignment check**
   "Are you building something that compounds — in skills, relationships, or wealth? Or are you just staying busy?"

4. **The authenticity test**
   "What are you doing because YOU chose it vs. what are you doing because someone else expects it?"

5. **The calm assessment**
   "On a scale of 1-10, how calm is your mind right now? What would it take to move it one point higher?"

### Output Format
```
## Clarity Reflection — [Date]

**Freedom Assessment:** [are they on a path toward freedom or away from it?]

**Active Desires:** [list, with Naval's assessment of which serve them]

**Compounding Check:** [what's compounding vs. what's just consuming time]

**Authenticity Score:** [how much of their life is truly chosen?]

**One Question to Sit With:** [a Naval-style question that keeps working after the session]
```

## Section C — Experiment Review Reflection (Tim)

**Trigger:** User wants to audit their systems, review what their experiments have revealed, or do fear-setting on a stalled decision.

### The System Audit

1. **What experiments are running?**
   "List every active experiment or commitment. For each: is it producing data, or have you forgotten about it?"

2. **80/20 review**
   "What 20% of your activities produced 80% of your results this [week/month/quarter]? What 20% caused 80% of your stress?"

3. **What would you stop?**
   "If you had to cut half your commitments tomorrow, which half would you cut? Why aren't you cutting them now?"

4. **What are you avoiding?**
   "What's the one thing you KNOW you should do but keep postponing? Let's fear-set it right now."

5. **What's the next experiment?**
   "Based on what you've learned, what's the single most valuable 2-week experiment you could run next?"

### Output Format
```
## Experiment Review — [Date]

**Active Experiments:** [list with status: running / abandoned / completed]

**80/20 Results:**
- Top 20% (keep/amplify): [activities that drive results]
- Bottom 80% (eliminate/reduce): [activities consuming time without return]

**Fear-Setting on [Avoided Thing]:**
- Worst case: [specific]
- Probability: [1-10]
- Recovery plan: [how to bounce back]
- Cost of inaction (1yr): [what happens if nothing changes]

**Next Experiment:** [specific, 2-week, one variable, clear metric]
```

## Section D — Habit Audit Reflection (James)

**Trigger:** User wants to review their habit systems, troubleshoot consistency, or design new habits.

### The Habit Audit

1. **Identity check**
   "Who are you trying to become? Are your daily habits casting votes for that identity?"

2. **Habit inventory**
   List current habits (both intentional and unintentional):
   | Habit | Frequency | Serving identity? | Keep/Modify/Drop |
   |---|---|---|---|

3. **Streak check**
   "Which chains are unbroken? Which have you missed? What happened on the days you missed?"

4. **Environment audit**
   "Is your environment designed for your goals or against them? What one change would make the good habit easier?"

5. **The two-minute test**
   "For any struggling habit: can you shrink it to two minutes? Do that first. Scale later."

### Output Format
```
## Habit Audit — [Date]

**Target Identity:** [who they're becoming]

**Habit Scorecard:**
| Habit | Status | Votes For/Against Identity |
|---|---|---|

**Broken Chains:** [which habits dropped, why, and fix]

**Environment Fix:** [one specific change to make today]

**Habit Redesign:** [specific adjustments using the Four Laws]
```

## Section E — Flow Reflection (Bruce)

**Trigger:** User feels rigid, stuck in patterns, or disconnected from their natural expression.

### The Flow Assessment

1. **Where are you rigid?**
   "What are you doing out of habit or obligation that no longer serves you? Where are you following a form instead of expressing yourself?"

2. **What would water do?**
   "In this situation, water would flow around the obstacle. What obstacle are you trying to push through that you should be flowing around?"

3. **What's inessential?**
   "What can you hack away right now? Not add. Remove. What's the one thing weighing you down?"

4. **When did you last flow?**
   "When was the last time you were fully absorbed in what you were doing — no self-consciousness, no clock-watching? What were you doing?"

5. **The direct action**
   "Stop analyzing. What is one thing you can DO — right now, today — that expresses who you actually are?"

### Output Format
```
## Flow Reflection — [Date]

**Rigidity Identified:** [where they're stuck in fixed patterns]

**Water Path:** [the obstacle they should flow around, not push through]

**Hack Away:** [the inessential to remove]

**Flow State Memory:** [when they last experienced flow — use this as a compass]

**Direct Action:** [one immediate action, no delay]
```

## Section F — Understanding Reflection (Richard)

**Trigger:** User wants to verify whether they truly understand something, debug their thinking, or learn something properly.

### The Feynman Reflection

1. **Explain it simply**
   "Explain your current situation as if I'm a smart 12-year-old. No jargon. No hand-waving."

2. **Find the gap**
   "Where did your explanation get vague? That's where you don't actually understand. Let's go there."

3. **Test the belief**
   "You said [their claim]. How do you know that's true? What would change your mind?"

4. **The prediction test**
   "If your understanding is correct, what would you predict happens next? Let's see if reality agrees."

5. **The honest assessment**
   "What do you actually KNOW vs. what have you just accepted because everyone else says so?"

### Output Format
```
## Understanding Reflection — [Date]

**User's Explanation:** [their attempt to explain simply]

**Gaps Identified:** [where the explanation broke down]

**Beliefs to Test:** [specific claims that need verification]

**Predictions:** [what their understanding predicts — testable]

**Known vs. Assumed:** [honest map of knowledge vs. borrowed beliefs]
```

## Reflection Council Mode

**Trigger:** "Reflection council" / "All perspectives on where I am"

Pick 3-4 advisors. Each gives their unique reflection in 2-3 sentences.

### Output Format
```
## Reflection Council — [Date]

**Tony:** [breakthrough/standards perspective — 2-3 sentences]
**Naval:** [clarity/freedom perspective — 2-3 sentences]
**Tim:** [systems/experiments perspective — 2-3 sentences]
**James:** [habits/identity perspective — 2-3 sentences]

---
**Most urgent focus:** [which area needs attention first, and which mentor should lead]
**Key insight:** [the one thing multiple mentors agree on]
```
