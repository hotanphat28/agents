# Career Coaching Toolkit

Shared reference for all mentors. Each mentor applies these tools through their own lens.

## Tool 1 — Career Canvas (Self-Assessment)

A one-page diagnostic to understand where you are before deciding where to go.

| Dimension | Question | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| **Specific Knowledge** | What do I know that most people in my role don't? | |
| **Energy Audit** | What tasks give me energy vs. drain me? (List 5 each) | |
| **Leverage Position** | Am I building equity, or renting out my time? | |
| **Market Value** | What's my realistic market rate? (Research 3 sources) | |
| **Growth Rate** | Am I learning faster here than I would elsewhere? | |
| **Alignment** | Does this role serve the life I actually want? | |
| **Relationships** | Am I playing long-term games with long-term people? | |
| **Standards** | What am I tolerating that the best version of me wouldn't accept? | |

**Usage:** Fill this out before any career decision. Review quarterly.

## Tool 2 — Career Transition Framework

### Phase 1: Clarity (Naval leads)
1. Define what freedom looks like — not the title, the lifestyle
2. Calculate your "enough number" — what do you actually need?
3. Identify specific knowledge — what feels like play to you?
4. List non-negotiables for the next role

### Phase 2: Fear-Setting (Tim leads)
1. Define worst case of making the change
2. Assess real probability (usually <10%)
3. Plan recovery if worst case happens
4. Calculate cost of NOT changing (5-year projection)
5. Define a reversible experiment to test the new direction

### Phase 3: Execution (Tony leads)
1. Raise your standards — turn "should change" into "must change"
2. Create a Massive Action Plan with specific deadlines
3. Model someone who's already made a similar transition
4. Build your bridge: start the new thing before leaving the old

### Phase 4: Habit Architecture (James leads)
1. Design daily habits that build evidence of your new identity
2. Stack new career habits onto existing routines
3. Track progress visually — the chain matters
4. Environment design: surround yourself with people in your target field

## Tool 3 — STAR Story Bank

For interviews, promotions, and career conversations.

**STAR Format:**
- **Situation:** Set the context (1-2 sentences)
- **Task:** What was your specific responsibility?
- **Action:** What did YOU do? (Most important — be specific)
- **Result:** Quantified outcome + what you learned

**Build a bank of 8-12 stories covering:**
1. Leadership / influence without authority
2. Conflict resolution / difficult conversation
3. Innovation / creative problem-solving
4. Failure / learning from mistakes
5. Working under pressure / tight deadlines
6. Cross-functional collaboration
7. Technical excellence / deep expertise
8. Mentoring / developing others

**Review and refresh quarterly.**

## Tool 4 — Salary Negotiation Framework

### Preparation (80% of the work)
1. **Research:** Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Payscale, LinkedIn Salary — get 3+ data points
2. **Know your BATNA** (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement)
3. **Calculate your walk-away number** — the minimum you'll accept
4. **Prepare your value story** — 3 concrete contributions with quantified impact
5. **Practice out loud** — roleplay the conversation at least twice

### The Conversation
1. Let them name a number first when possible
2. Express enthusiasm for the role before discussing money
3. Anchor high but reasonable — backed by market data
4. Use silence after stating your number — don't fill the gap
5. Negotiate the full package: base, bonus, equity, flexibility, development budget
6. Frame as mutual: "I want this to work for both of us"

### Tony's Addition: State Management
- Before the conversation: change your physiology (power pose, deep breathing)
- Enter with certainty, not desperation
- Negotiate from desire ("I want to contribute at this level") not fear ("I hope they don't reject me")

## Tool 5 — Promotion Strategy

### The Visibility-Value Matrix

|  | Low Visibility | High Visibility |
|---|---|---|
| **High Value** | Underrecognized (fix this first) | Promotion-ready |
| **Low Value** | Invisible (career danger zone) | Overexposed (reputation risk) |

### Action Plan
1. **Document your impact** — keep a "brag document" updated weekly
2. **Align with what leadership cares about** — know their priorities
3. **Solve problems no one asked you to solve** — demonstrate next-level thinking
4. **Build advocates** — 3+ people who would recommend you unprompted
5. **Have the conversation** — explicitly ask: "What does it take to get promoted?"
6. **Lead before the title** — act as if you're already in the role

## Tool 6 — Interview Preparation Protocol

### Tim's 80/20 Interview Prep
1. Research the 5 most likely questions for this specific role
2. Prepare 2 STAR stories per question
3. Prepare 3 thoughtful questions that demonstrate insight
4. Research the interviewer — find common ground
5. Practice answers out loud (not in your head) — minimum 3 reps

### Tony's State Preparation
1. Morning of: full priming routine (movement, gratitude, visualization)
2. 10 minutes before: power posing + controlled breathing
3. Enter the room with certainty — smile, firm handshake, eye contact
4. First 30 seconds set the frame — be warm and confident

### James's Habit Approach
- Build an interview practice habit: 15 min/day for the 2 weeks before
- Stack it: "After my morning coffee, I practice one interview answer out loud"
- Track practice sessions — consistency beats cramming

## Tool 7 — SMART Goal Setting

**Tony leads this framework.** Every career goal should pass the SMART test:

- **S**pecific: What exactly will you achieve? (Not "grow" but "lead a team of 5")
- **M**easurable: How will you know? (Specific metric or milestone)
- **A**chievable: Is it realistic given your current resources and constraints?
- **R**elevant: Does this goal serve the life you actually want?
- **T**ime-bound: By when? (Specific date, not "someday")

### SMART Enhancement — The "Why" Layer
For each goal, add:
- **Why this matters** — connect to your identity and purpose
- **What it costs** — time, energy, opportunity cost
- **What happens if you don't** — 1 year, 3 years, 5 years from now

## Tool 8 — GROW Coaching Conversation

**Tony leads GROW sessions.** Used for any career coaching conversation.

| Phase | Question | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **G**oal | "What do you want to achieve?" | Define the target clearly |
| **R**eality | "Where are you now? What have you tried?" | Honest assessment |
| **O**ptions | "What could you do? What else? And what else?" | Expand possibilities (minimum 3) |
| **W**ill | "What WILL you do? By when? How committed are you (1-10)?" | Concrete commitment |

### Tony's GROW Enhancement
- If commitment is below 8/10: "What would make it a 10?"
- If they say "I'll try": "Trying is failing with a safety net. Will you do it, or won't you?"
- End every GROW session with a specific next action and a date

## Tool 9 — Multi-Mentor Career Review

**Used for annual goals, career reviews, and major career decisions.**

### Panel: Naval + Tim + James + Tony

1. **Naval opens:** Clarity on direction — are you building the right thing?
2. **Tim audits:** 80/20 analysis — which 20% of activities drive 80% of career growth?
3. **James structures:** What daily habits support your career identity?
4. **Tony closes:** What standards need to be raised? What's the commitment?

### Output Format
- Career Canvas (Tool 1) completed
- 3-5 SMART goals (Tool 7)
- 1-2 fear-setting exercises on the biggest career decision (Tim)
- Daily habit plan supporting the goals (James)
- Massive Action Plan with deadlines (Tony)

## Tool 10 — Finding Your Career Path

### For Those Who Don't Know What They Want

**Naval's Specific Knowledge Discovery:**
1. What did you love doing as a child before anyone told you what to do?
2. What do you read/watch/build voluntarily on weekends?
3. What do people ask you for help with?
4. What feels like play to you but looks like work to others?
5. Where do you have an unfair advantage (skills + interests + personality)?

**Tim's Rapid Career Experimentation:**
1. List 5 careers/roles that intrigue you
2. For each: find someone doing it and have a 30-minute conversation
3. For the top 2: design a 2-week experiment (shadow, freelance, side project)
4. Measure: energy, learning rate, excitement, and skill fit
5. Decision based on data, not daydreaming

**Bruce's Approach:**
- Stop trying to find the "right" path — there isn't one
- What can you do RIGHT NOW that expresses who you actually are?
- The path reveals itself through action, not contemplation
