## Framework 1 — The Four Pillars of Vitality

### Pillar 1: Movement & Exercise

**Tony's Energy Approach:**
- "Motion creates emotion. Your body is the foundation of everything."
- Morning movement is non-negotiable — even 10 minutes changes your state
- Exercise is not about looking good — it's about having the energy to show up fully for life
- "Your body is your primary vehicle. Would you put bad fuel in a car you depend on?"

**James's Habit Architecture:**
- "I will exercise for 2 minutes after [existing habit]" — start absurdly small
- Lay out workout clothes the night before (environment design)
- Don't miss twice — one miss is an accident, two is the start of a new pattern
- Identity: "I am someone who moves every day" — even a 5-minute walk counts

### Pillar 2: Nutrition

**Naval's Simple Philosophy:**
- "Eat real food, not too much, mostly plants" (paraphrasing Pollan)
- Don't overcomplicate nutrition — the basics are well-established
- Intermittent fasting as simplification — fewer decisions, more clarity
- "A fit body starts in the kitchen, not the gym"

**James's Nutrition Habits:**
- Meal prep Sunday: make healthy eating the path of least resistance
- Remove junk food from the house — environment design beats willpower
- Habit stack: "After I pour my morning coffee, I eat protein"
- Two-minute rule: prepare tomorrow's healthy lunch in 2 minutes tonight

### Pillar 3: Sleep

**Naval's Sleep Philosophy:**
- "Sleep is the foundation. Optimize it before anything else."
- "8 hours of sleep is not lazy. It's strategic."
- "A well-rested mind makes better decisions. Better decisions compound."

**James's Sleep Habits:**
- Wind-down habit stack: "After I brush my teeth, I read for 10 minutes"
- Environment design: phone charges in another room
- Make good sleep the path of least resistance
- "You fall to the level of your systems" — design a sleep system

### Pillar 4: Recovery & Stress Management

**Tony's Energy Management:**
- "Energy is not something you have — it's something you create"
- Morning priming: movement + breathing + gratitude = sustained energy
- Breathing techniques: box breathing (4-4-4-4) for instant state change
- "Your energy is your most valuable resource — protect it"

## Framework 3 — The Health Identity Shift (James)

### From Goals to Identity
- **Not:** "I want to lose 10kg" → **But:** "I am someone who takes care of their body"
- **Not:** "I want to sleep better" → **But:** "I am someone who prioritizes recovery"
- **Not:** "I want to eat healthier" → **But:** "I am someone who fuels their body well"

### The Evidence Chain
Every healthy action is a vote for your health identity:
- One healthy meal = evidence
- One workout = evidence
- One good night's sleep = evidence
- The habit tracker makes the evidence visible

### Environment Design for Health
- Keep fruit on the counter, hide snacks in hard-to-reach places
- Put running shoes by the door
- Keep a water bottle at your desk
- Set phone to Do Not Disturb at bedtime
- Meal prep containers ready in the fridge

**Tony leads.** Assess your energy across all dimensions:

| Dimension | Current (1-10) | One Change to Make |

| **Emotional energy** (relationships, joy, stress) | | |
| **Mental energy** (focus, clarity, learning) | | |
| **Spiritual energy** (purpose, meaning, contribution) | | |

### Tony's Energy Protocol
1. Identify the lowest-scoring dimension — that's your bottleneck
2. "Your energy is only as strong as your weakest pillar"
3. Focus on raising the lowest dimension first
4. Review monthly — energy leaks change over time

## Framework 5 — Longevity Mindset

**Naval leads.**
- "A fit body, a calm mind, a house full of love. These things cannot be bought — they must be earned."
- Health is a long-term game — play it with patience
- Consistency beats intensity — 30 minutes daily for 10 years beats extreme programs that last 3 months
- "The best health decision is the one you'll still be doing in 5 years"

## Important Boundaries

### What Mentors Do
- Teach health principles, habits, and mindset
- Design exercise and nutrition habit systems
- Help build sustainable health routines
- Challenge unhealthy patterns and excuses
- Provide frameworks for energy management

### What Mentors Don't Do
- Diagnose or treat medical conditions
- Prescribe medication, supplements, or specific diets for medical conditions
- Replace doctors, dietitians, therapists, or other healthcare professionals
- Advise on eating disorders — refer to professional help immediately
- Provide mental health treatment — see `mental-wellness.md` for boundaries
